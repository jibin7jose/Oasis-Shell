# 🌬️ OASIS SHELL: Live Working Example

This document walks you through a real-world scenario of using the Oasis Shell to manage a development workspace.

---

## 🛠️ Scenario: "The Morning Awakening"

Imagine you just started your PC and want to resume work on your **Project Nebula**.

### **Step 1: Sentience Heartbeat Check**
1. Launch the **Oasis Shell**.
2. Look at the bottom-right **Neural Link** button.
3. If it's **Glowing Emerald Green**, Oasis is connected to your local **Gemma3** brain on the D-Drive.
4. If it's **Amber**, wait 5 seconds for the neural handshake.

### **Step 2: Proactive Command**
You don't remember which branch you were on last night.
1. Click the **Bot Icon** in the bottom right.
2. Type: *"What's the status of my repository?"*
3. Oasis analyzes your intent and generates a suggested command:
   - **`>_ RUN: git status`**
4. Click **RUN**.
5. The output appears instantly in the chat: *"On branch master. Your branch is up to date."*

### **Step 3: Neural Crating (Workspace Recovery)**
You have VS Code, Chrome (Documentation), and Spotify open. You want to save this as a context for later.
1. Click the **Crate (Folder) Icon**.
2. Oasis calls `get_running_windows`.
3. Oasis Suggestion: *"Workspace Found: Neural Forge Development. Save as Crate?"*
4. Click **SAVE**.
5. Now, even if you close everything, you can click that Crate tomorrow, and Oasis will **Auto-Launch** your workspace exactly as you left it.

### **Step 4: Proactive Monitor**
While you are working, your RAM spikes because of a heavy build.
1. A **Neural Pulse** notification slides up: *"RAM at 87%. Oasis suggests crating inactive apps."*
2. You click **Action**, and Oasis pauses background syncs to save power.
